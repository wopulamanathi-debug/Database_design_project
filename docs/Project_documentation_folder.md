Business Narrative 

Legacy Stationery was created to make student life easier by providing affordable and convenient 
access to stationery and study essentials through an online platform. Many students face financial 
pressures such as tuition, transport, and accommodation costs, making it difficult to afford basic 
academic tools. Legacy Stationery aims to reduce this burden by offering cost-effective, high-quality 
products that support student success. 

Operating in the e-commerce industry, the platform allows students to shop anytime and from 
anywhere, saving time and reducing stress. Our product range includes general stationery, study 
accessories, electronics, and specialised academic equipment such as calculators, lab coats, drawing 
boards, and design kits. 

What makes Legacy Stationery unique is its student-focused approach. We offer competitive prices, 
student package deals, payment plans for expensive items, and delivery services to campuses such as 
University of Johannesburg, University of the Witwatersrand, University of Pretoria, and Tshwane 
University of Technology. Our goal is to ensure students can focus on their studies without worrying 
about accessing essential learning materials. 

Unique Business Rules 

1. A customer can have a subscription. A subscription is linked to one customer.  
2. Discount vouchers must be linked to a valid student account and cannot be reused. 
3. Free delivery is offered for orders above a certain amount, and each qualifying order is linked 
to one free delivery. 
4. A supplier may participate in bundle deals, where products are grouped for student 
discounts. A bundle deal can include products from one or more suppliers.

Assumptions 

1. The demand for stationery and study materials from university students is assumed to be 
consistent throughout the academic year.  
2. Students will prefer online shopping over physical stores due to convenience and time 
constraints. 
3. All listed universities (UJ, Wits, UP, TUT) are assumed to have active student populations that 
regularly require stationery supplies. 
4. Product suppliers are assumed to be reliable and able to restock items when inventory runs 
low. 
5. Students are assumed to be price-sensitive and more likely to choose affordable or 
discounted products.
